# Brain Cells Playing DOOM

Researchers recently demonstrated something both fascinating and slightly unsettling:  
human brain cells grown in a petri dish have learned how to play the classic game **DOOM**.

https://x.com/MAstronomers/status/2030294901695361444?s=20

A video demonstration can be seen here:

Living Human Brain Cells Play DOOM on CL1  
https://youtu.be/yRV8fSw6HaE?si=ZN3OcIc0eiWTPtRN

---

According to the report, approximately **200,000 human neurons** were cultured on a chip placed inside a glass dish.  
These neurons were connected to electrodes that allowed them to both **receive sensory input** and **generate output signals**.

In the experiment:

- electrical signals represented the position of enemies in the game  
- the neurons interpreted those signals as sensory input  
- the neural culture learned to generate spikes that triggered the in-game firing action

In other words, the neurons gradually learned to interact with the game environment through a feedback loop.

---

What makes the experiment strange is the conceptual image it creates.

Inside a glass dish, a cluster of neurons exists in isolation from the real world.  
Their only interaction with reality is an endless loop of **DOOM**.

They perceive enemies.  
They fire.  
They die.  
They respawn.

Again and again.

All of this happens in real time, as the neural network continuously adapts to the feedback it receives.

---

So what should we call this existence?

Is it a kind of **endless punishment**, a technological version of *Avici*, the Buddhist hell of eternal suffering?

Or is it closer to **Valhalla**, where warriors fight and fall endlessly in an eternal battle?

Perhaps it is neither.

Perhaps it is simply another reminder that the boundary between **biological intelligence and machines** is becoming less clear than we once imagined.
