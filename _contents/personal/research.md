# Research

My research focuses on the intersection of **embedded systems, robotics platforms, and high-performance computing**.  
I study how complex hardware–software systems behave under real-world constraints, particularly where reliability, timing behavior, and system architecture interact.

My work connects **academic system modeling with production-grade embedded platforms**, including Linux-based SoCs, UAV systems, and GPU-accelerated simulation environments.

---

## Research Areas

### Embedded and Real-Time Systems

My work in embedded systems focuses on reliability, system architecture, and performance behavior in Linux-based platforms.

Research topics include:

- Linux kernel debugging and device driver development
- Power management and system bring-up for ARM-based embedded platforms
- Timing behavior and reliability in real-time and near-real-time systems
- Long-term maintainability of production embedded platforms

This work is motivated by the challenges of deploying embedded systems that must operate reliably under long-term operational constraints.

---

### Robotics Middleware and UAV Systems

Another major research direction involves robotics middleware and UAV platforms.

My work explores how robotics software frameworks interact with embedded systems and real-world hardware constraints.

Research topics include:

- ROS 2 middleware integration with embedded platforms
- UAV control architectures and robotics system integration
- Real-time data pipelines and sensor integration
- Wireless communication systems including private 5G networks

These systems require careful coordination between software architecture, hardware capabilities, and communication constraints.

---

### GPU-Accelerated Simulation and Performance Modeling

My doctoral research focused on improving the scalability of large-scale traffic simulations.

Research topics include:

- CUDA-based parallel traffic simulation models
- Distributed simulation architectures using OpenMPI
- Empirical performance measurement of parallel systems
- Performance prediction models for simulation workloads

This work aims to understand how parallel computing platforms can support complex simulation environments used in urban planning and transportation research.

---

## Research Approach

My research combines **system design, empirical experimentation, and performance analysis**.

The typical workflow includes:

1. Designing system architectures that expose measurable behavior  
2. Building experimental prototypes on real platforms  
3. Measuring system behavior using profiling and benchmarking tools  
4. Iteratively refining system designs based on empirical results  

This approach allows research ideas to be validated under realistic operating conditions rather than purely theoretical assumptions.

---

## Current Directions

My current work explores the behavior of **complex hardware–software stacks** where embedded devices, robotics systems, and large-scale computation interact.

Current interests include:

- Observability and performance behavior in ROS 2 communication systems  
- Reliability and debugging of Linux-based embedded platforms  
- Integration of robotics middleware with real-world hardware constraints  
- Scalable simulation systems for complex environments  

---

## Research Philosophy

I believe good systems research should remain closely connected to real systems.

Rather than studying abstract models alone, my work emphasizes **building and measuring actual systems**.  
This allows research results to remain relevant to both academic inquiry and industrial engineering practice.
